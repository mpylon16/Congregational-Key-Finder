ℹ️ About the analysis

This tool analyzes your uploaded melody to recommend the best key for congregational singing. It uses research-informed vocal ranges to assess how singable the melody is for a typical group of untrained voices.

🎶 What’s considered comfortable?

  • 🎯 Ideal Tessitura: C4–C5 — where most voices can sing easily for long periods
  • ✅ Comfortable Range: A3–D5 — safe for nearly all adults in mixed groups
  • ⚠️ Stretch Zone: G3–A3 or D5–E5 — singable, but may strain some voices
  • ❌ Out of Range: below G3 or above E5 — difficult or unreachable for most

🎯 How the Comfort Score works

Each note is scored based on its pitch and duration. Notes outside the ideal range increase the score:

  • 0 penalty: Notes between C4–C5
  • +1 per beat: Slightly outside (e.g., A3–B3, C5–D5)
  • +3 per beat: Edge of range (e.g., G3, D#5–E5)
  • +10 per beat: Far out of range (e.g., F3 or F5+)

📝 Notes

  • Only notes with lyrics are analyzed — instrumental intros/outros are ignored.
  • Accidentals and key signatures do not affect comfort score — only pitch and duration matter.
  • Key recommendations aim to bring the melody into the most comfortable range with the fewest awkward leaps.
