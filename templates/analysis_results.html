💡 How Options are Evaluated & Ranked

Singability is evaluated based on vocal brackets for standard untrained congregations. The further away from the ideal range and the more sustained a note is, the harder it is to sing and the higher penalty it applies to the singability score.

Singability is averaged across the whole song, giving a percentage from 0 (totally unsingable) to 100 (all notes within the ideal range). After categorising, keys are then sorted by balancing singability (vocal comfort) against playability (how much of a pain it is to play for the band). Points are subtracted from the vocal score if a key uses difficult accidentals (like 5 sharps) or awkward fretboard/valve positions for typical worship instruments.
